Raised in Hillsborough, North Carolina, just outside of the Research Triangle Park, I have always had a natural affinity for science and mathematics. Trained as a chemist at Harvard University, I initially sought a career in the research sciences with a focus on protein biochemistry. However, a few years into completing my PhD in Biophysics at Stanford University, I realized that bench science was not the path I wished to pursue. I had always been curious about computer science, so I decided to enroll in a class at Stanford for some extracurricular fun. 3 courses later, I realized I enjoyed the rigorous problem solving and creativity that software engineering requires. After graduating, I became a Google Tech Career Training Fellow to further build upon my existing coding base and enter the tech field.

Although I like to work hard, I also have a wide variety of hobbies. As the daughter of an immigrant, I know how important it is to have a global understanding of the world and all of its people. I love to travel and have been to over 15 countries, including China, Germany, Trinidad & Tobago, and Morocco. I spent a year teaching English in Spain and spent a week in Beijing competing in a high school science competition.

I love hiking and running long distances. After graduating with my PhD in 2021, I spent 5 months on the Continental Divide Trail hiking from Canada to Mexico, with a large portion of the trip spent hiking alone. I climbed countless mountains, saw 4 bears (including 2 grizzly bears), and made enough memories to last a lifetime. In March of 2022, I completed my first marathon and have plans to do many more.

As a creative person, I love engaging in the arts. During 2020, I taught myself oil painting and digital art. I also enjoy playing the electric guitar and attending live concerts.
